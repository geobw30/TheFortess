Our Vision

The Fortress is a non-profit ( Non Governmental Organisation) committed to championing the cause of the girl child and women: enabling them to overcome their odds and maximize their potential, transforming their lives and as a result, their families and communities. We work with vulnerable girls, mothers, victims of crisis/ teenage pregnancy, child marriage, Gender Based Violance(GBV), abuse as well as HIV AIDS. We rehabilitate, rescue, empower, train in skills and provide counselling and unconditional love! By investing in the girl child and women today, we are building a brighter future for everyone tomorrow!!

Stories

Some of our stories

“Prevention is better than cure” is a very old saying, but when you really think about it — it’s absolutely true. At The Fortress, we’ve witnessed the transformation of every young mother we've rescued, rehabilitated, and empowered in various ways. However, we've also realized that prevention is just as critical. That’s why we’ve committed to proactive efforts in preventing teenage pregnancy, child marriage, gender-based violence, and abuse. We are actively conducting training sessions and empowerment programs targeting girls at risk — especially in vulnerable environments, primary and secondary schools, and rural communities. We’re already seeing the fruits of this work, and it inspires us to reach even more girls — and boys too! A few days ago, while visiting a particular school, I was struck by how naïve and unaware some students are about topics like reproductive health, mental health, and self-awareness — things we often take for granted. We also spoke about purpose — encouraging these young stars to look beyond their current challenges and focus on their gifts, potential, and the bigger picture of their future. What a beautiful and impactful training it was!

Prevention is better than cure indeed!

I was born into a Muslim family and lived as a Muslim my entire life—until I was rescued by The Fortress. My childhood was marked by hardship. At just seven years old, I was raped by my uncle, who was HIV-positive. By God’s grace, I escaped contracting the virus. My mother, overwhelmed by work, had little time for us, and my father, who had multiple wives, neglected his responsibilities. As a result, my siblings and I grew up fending for ourselves. In my senior year of high school, I managed to pass my exams, but the challenges only grew. My mother could no longer afford my school fees, forcing me to stay home for a year. During that time, anger consumed me, and I fell into bad company. Peer pressure led me to alcohol and drugs, and a boy deceived me into believing he loved me. He got me pregnant and then abandoned me, revealing his true nature. When my mother found out, she pressured me to abort the baby. But something inside me gave me the strength to refuse. Life became even harder—every morning, I woke up to her anger and disappointment. At just 17, with no support and struggling to survive, I felt utterly lost… until I found The Fortress. There, my life transformed. I found peace, unconditional love, counseling, and care. For the first time, I had food, medical support, and a safe place to stay. Most importantly, I encountered God and came to know Jesus. He performed a miracle in my life—I gave birth without pain, just as I had prayed. At The Fortress, I learned handcraft skills, which allowed me to leave with some savings to start a new life. I named my son Shalom ("peace") because that’s what I found there. They provided baby clothes, a mama kit for safe delivery, and all the support I desperately needed. After months of healing, The Fortress reunited me with my family through counseling. Though life remained difficult, I faced society’s judgment with strength—prepared by the resilience I had gained. My mother, who once resented my pregnancy, softened when she saw my innocent baby boy. She even named him Raymond. But my father disowned me when he learned I had become a born-again Christian. Determined to change my life, I embraced the vision Mama Fortress, Josephine, had for us—to become women of purpose. Today, thanks to The Fortress and another organization, I am studying Nursing at university—my dream! My son is in school, living with my mother. I now serve as an usher in my church, growing deeper in faith. All this was possible because of The Fortress’s love and empowerment. To the staff and sponsors—your giving has given me a future! When I graduate, I hope to help many others as a nurse and build a better life. Long live The Fortress—more girls like me need you.

Dream come true!

PPart of our National Anthem says: “Oh Uganda, the land that feeds us, with sun and fertile crops grown…” Now that we are older, we truly realize how blessed Uganda is — with its beautiful weather and fertile soil. At The Fortress, we embrace this blessing by growing vegetables, maize, sugarcane, beans, and planting several fruit trees. We are now beginning to enjoy the fruits of our labor — including pawpaws, mangoes, avocados, and the famous jackfruit. In this photo is one of our joyful young mothers at The Fortress, celebrating a bountiful maize harvest. Very soon, we’ll be enjoying porridge and posho made from it! Consider visiting us — come see and be a part of this life-giving experience!
